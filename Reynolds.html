This Reynolds number (Re) calculator describes how fluid will flow in a pipe. Based on the value, the fluid flow can be charaterized into one of three flow regimes: Laminar, Transition, and Turbulent.

  • Laminar Flow Regime Re < 2000: A bullet shaped flow profile. The fluid in the center of the pipe flows the fastest and the flow near the pipe wall flows the slowest.
  • Transition Flow Regime 2000 > Re > 4000: A poorly charaterized flow regime which is neither laminar nor turbulent.
  • Turbulent Flow Regime Re > 4000: A flat line shaped flow profile. The fluid in the center of the pipe swirls with the edge flow and they both move at relatively the same speed.

This Reynolds number is a function of pipe geometery, flow rate, fluid density, and fluid viscosity

\[Re = \frac{u * d_i * \rho}{\mu}\]

The inner pipe diameter can be found using the outer diamter and pipe thickness

\[d_i = d_o - 2* x_w\]

The fluid velocity can be found using fluid flow rate and pipe geometery

\[v = Q / A_i\]

Definitions

\(v\) = Fluid Velocity
Q = Fluid Flow Rate
\(d_o\) = Pipe Outer Diameter
\(d_i\) = Pipe Inner Diameter
\(x_w\) = Pipe wall thickness
\(r_i\) = Pipe Inner Radius
\(A_i\) = Pipe Inner Cross-sectional Area
ρ = Fluid Density
μ = Fluid Dynamic Viscosity
Re = Reynolds Dimentionless Number

Find the flow regime for water (1000 kg/m³ and 1 cP) in a 1.5" OD tube (0.065" wall thickness) flowing at 60lpm.

Calculate inner pipe diameter: \[ d_i = d_o - 2 \times x_w\] \[ d_i = 1.5" - 2\times0.065" = 1.37\text{ in}\] \[ d_i = 1.37\text{ in} \times \frac{1\text{ ft}}{12\text{ in} } = 0.114 \text{ ft}\] Calculate inner pipe area: \[A_i = \pi \times r_i^2 = \pi \times \left(\frac{d_i}{2}\right)^2 \] \[A_i = \pi \times \left(\frac{0.114\text{ ft}}{2}\right)^2 =0.01\text{ ft²} \] Calculate fluid velocity: \[v = \frac{Q}{A_i}\] \[v = \frac{60 \frac{L}{min} \times \frac{1 \text{ft³}}{28.3 \text{L}} \times \frac{60 sec}{min} }{0.01\text{ ft²}} = 3.45 \text{ ft/s}\] Calculate Reynolds number: \[ Re = \frac{v \times d_i \times \rho}{\mu}\] \[ \rho = 1000 \text{ kg/m³} \times \frac{1 \text{lb/ft³}}{16.02 \text{ kg/m³}} = 62.4 \text{lb/ft³} \] \[ \mu = 1 \text{ cP} \times \frac{\text{lb/ft/s}}{1488.16 \text{ cP}} = 0.00067 \text{lb/ft/s} \] \[ Re = \frac{3.45 \text{ ft/s} \times 0.114 \text{ ft}\ \times 62.4 \text{lb/ft³}} {0.00067 \text{lb/ft/s}} \] \[ Re = \text{36,589} \] Determine flow regime: \[ \text{Flow Regime = 36,589 > 4,000 = Turbulent} \]

Since the Reynolds number determines the charateristics of fluid flow, it is typically used as a precursory calculation in many fluid dynamic and heat transfer calcuations.

Fluid Mixing

If the goal for an application is to acchieve high mixing, ensure the reynolds number is in the turbulent regime. Turbulent flow helps pipe cleaning during Clean-In-Place (CIP) of sanitary equipment.

Heat Transfer

Turbulent flow will transfer heat more effiecntly than laminar flow. Heat exchangers aim for turbulent flow within the tubes and shell.
